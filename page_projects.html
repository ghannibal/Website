Suspicious of Artificial Intelligence (SUSAI)

#Principle Investigator

Extensive theoretical and empirical work has enabled the human-AI interaction (HAII) research community to understand what makes people trust in AI. In current research, trust in Artificial Intelligence (AI) is strongly encouraged because the benefits of trusting are often overemphasized. This bias of optimism comes at the expense of a more balanced and complete perspective on the nature and the eﬀects of trust because it is also important to consider the so-called “dark side” . Much less effort is devoted to developing a good understanding of mistrust and distrust although research has already shown that AI is also used for harmful and criminal activities. The topic of mistrust and distrust in AI is sparsely explored in the specific context of HAII. The proposed Suspicious of Artificial Intelligence (SUSAI) project aims to fill this research gap by investigating why people are unwilling to trust in AI by focusing on the broader notion of mistrust. Using the methods of a literature review and expert interviews, the SUSAI project intends to develop a conceptual model of mistrust in AI applicable to everyday life HAII contexts.

PI: Glenda Hannibal | Funder: Early Career Grant, University of Salzburg | Budget: 45.000 EUR | Duration: 2026-2027.

Understanding and Impacting Consumer Behavior Towards Food Sustainability (Nudge2Green)

#External Collaborater

Human behavior plays a pivotal role in food sustainability across the supply chain. Nudge2Green seeks to use behavior and food sciences, data-driven approaches, and advanced analytics to influence consumer behavior and promote sustainable food choices while reducing unhealthy preferences. The project will create a framework tested in two use cases: UC1, examining consumer behavior in physical markets, and UC2, exploring human factors in e-commerce. With a consortium of universities and companies, the project aims to deliver reusable software for organizations and advance knowledge in sustainable food and adaptive systems.

PI: Mahyar Moghaddam | Partners: University of Southen Denmark (lead), Aarhus University, University of Copenhagen, Food Organisation of Denmark, Heco et al ApS | Funder: AgriFoodTure Partnership, Innovation Fund Denmark | Budget: 10.700.000 DKK | Duration: 2025-2027.

Excellence in Digital Sciences and Interdisciplinary Technologies (EXDIGIT)

#Co-Investigator

The EXDIGIT project pursues the long-term vision of establishing Salzburg as a leading and internationally recognized hub for digital sciences and interdisciplinary technologies. EXDIGIT aims to serve as an innovative platform that combines technological excellence with social relevance. Its stated objective is to develop and apply digital key technologies such as Artificial Intelligence, Big Data, Geoinformatics or Human-Computer Interaction not in isolation, but in dynamic interaction with ethical, social, ecological, and economic perspectives, and to embed them into broader disciplinary contexts.

PI: Manfred Tscheligi | Partners: University of Salzburg (lead), Salzburg Research, Innovation Salzburg GmbH | Funder: Land Salzburg | Budget: 12.719.832 EUR | Duration: 2022-2028.

Exploring Long-Term Human-Robot Interaction (SharedSpace)

#Co-Investigator

The SharedSpace project aimed to study the interaction of ordinary users with the social companion robot, Anki Vector, over time and how it effected adoption and acceptance differ in terms of the socio-demographic qualities of the involved households.

PI: Astrid Weiss | Funder: FWF (Elise Richter programme) | Budget: 250.000 EUR | Duration: 2018-2022.
