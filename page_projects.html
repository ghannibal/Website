Excellence in Digital Sciences and Interdisciplinary Technologies

The Excellence in Digital Sciences and Interdisciplinary Technologies (EXDIGIT) project pursues the long-term vision of establishing Salzburg as a leading and internationally recognized hub for digital sciences and interdisciplinary technologies. EXDIGIT aims to serve as an innovative platform that combines technological excellence with social relevance. Its stated objective is to develop and apply digital key technologies such as Artificial Intelligence, Big Data, Geoinformatics or Human-Computer Interaction not in isolation, but in dynamic interaction with ethical, social, ecological, and economic perspectives, and to embed them into broader disciplinary contexts.

Role: Co-Investigator | PI: Manfred Tscheligi | Partners: University of Salzburg (lead), Salzburg Research, Innovation Salzburg GmbH | Funding: Land Sazlburg | Budget: 12.719.832 EUR | Duration: 2022-2028.

Shared Space: Exploring Long-Term Human-Robot Interaction

The Shared Space project aimed to study the interaction of ordinary users with the social companion robot, Anki Vector, over time and how it effected adoption and acceptance differ in terms of the socio-demographic qualities of the involved households.

Role: Co-Investigator | PI: Astrid Weiss | Funding: FWF (Elise Richter programme) | Budget: 250.000 EUR | Duration: 2018-2022.
