BIOGRAPHY

I am currently an independent Postdoctoral Researcher in the EXDIGIT project at the Department of Artificial Intelligence and Human Interfaces, University of Salzburg.

I was previously a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Artificial Intelligence at Ulm University (2022-2023) where I worked on combining my trust research with the topic of Explainable AI (XAI). While still a doctoral student, I served as an expert for the European Commission through my work in the HUMAINT project. Member of the TrustRobots Doctoral College (2018-2022) and the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) group at TU Wien, I obtained my Doctorate in Computer Science (2022) where I focused on the topic of (interpersonal) trust in human-robot interaction. You are welcome to read and download my dissertation, which I passed with distinction. I hold a BA and MA in Philosophy (2015) from Aarhus University where my thesis projects explored and presented the metaphysical dimensions of social robotics and also considered metaphilosophical perspectives.

I contribute actively to the international and national HCI research community, taking on a range of professional responsibilities. Notably, I have served as an Associated Chair in the subcommittee on Critical Computing, Susaintability, and Social Justice (CCSSJ) at the CHI conference in 2025 and 2026. I am also the Vice Chair of the Austrian ACM SIGCHI Chapter, which is a national research network dedicated to strengthening and coordinating HCI research within Austria. Furthermore, I am growing into a leadership role by taking on supervision responsibilities and supporting junior researchers in their development. In addition, I regularly serve as program committee member, associate chair, and reviewer for major conferences and journals. Through these activities, I help maintain quality standards, support colleagues, and contribute to shaping discussions in human-AI interaction research. Alongside my academic service, I have been engaged in science communication and public outreach, motivated by my strong commitment to sharing research insights with the general public. My public-facing activities include, for example, an online talk in the Talking Robotics seminar series and participation in the panel discussion held by the Deutsches Museum Bonn on the societal implications of AI and robots.

You can find the details in my academic CV.
