Topics

I have also been responsible for planning and conducting various courses and seminars, and below is a list of the various topics I have been covering.

Responsible Human-Computer Interaction

Institution: University of Salzburg (AT) | Programme: BSc on Artificial Intelligence | Language: English | 2 ECTS | Semester: SS 2026

This course explores the theory and methods of responsible HCI and examine these ideas through real-world examples of using and designing computer-based technologies. Responsible HCI focuses on integrating knowledge and approaches from a range of disciplines to anticipate and mitigate the negative social, ethical, and environmental impacts of computer-based technologies. Students will learn how to apply ethical frameworks, consider diverse perspectives, and reflect critically on the use and design of computer-based technologies to ensure they aligns with broader social and ethical values.

Advanced Contextual Interfaces

Institution: University of Salzburg/Salzburg University of Applied Sciences (AT) | Programme: Joint MSc on Human-Computer Interaction | Co-lecturer: M. Beuthel | Language: English | 3 ECTS | Semester: WS 2024/25, WS 2025/26

This course intensifies the knowledge of different types of contextual interfaces based on recent streams such as autonomous systems, social computing, persuasive technologies, ubiquitous computing, wearable computing or tangible interaction. Students will be focusing on one stream and conceptualise, design and prototype one or more advanced contextual interfaces for a specific application area. Students will be able to discuss various HCI streams. Based on one specific stream they will be able to solve contextual challenges and design one or more advanced contextual interfaces for a specific application area.

Explainable Artificial Intelligence

Institution: Ulm University (DE) | Program: BSc/MSc in Computer Science/MSc on Cognitive Systems | Language: English | 2 ECTS | Semester: WS 2022/23, SS 2023

In this seminar students will get introduced to the emerging topic of explainable AI (XAI) by considering both the more technical challenges and reflect on their advantage and disadvantages within a broader social and ethical debate. Students start by considering the core concepts and theories relevant for XAI from a philosophical perspective. Then they will focus on the various XAI algorithms and solution offered in the current literature to ensure that AI systems are explainable. Afterwards, students look at different domains of application in which XAI had been considered to learn about how this pose both opportunities and challenges. Throughout the entire seminar students are also learning how to critically reflect upon the social and ethical dimensions of XAI.

AI Ethics

Institution: Ulm University (DE) | Program: BSc/MSc in Computer Science/MSc on Cognitive Systems | Language: English | 2 ECTS | Semester: WS 2022/23, SS 2023

This seminar aims to make students familiar with the ways in which developments in Artificial Intelligence (AI) intersects. Students start out by taking a meta-ethical perspective to unpack the basic concepts and theories of ethics. Then they attend more specifically to the different ethical theories and their principles to better understand how they can support the design of ethical reasoning and behavior for AI systems. They continue by looking at several examples of how the ethical concerns raised depend on the specific domain of application and what has been done so far to address these concerns. Students throughout the entire seminar take into account how AI ethics overlap with the related themes and concepts of trust, accountability, fairness, explainability, privacy, and transparency.

​Diagnosis of Society - Social Robotics

Institution: University of Vienna (AT) | Programme: BSc in Sociology | Language: English | 2 ECTS | Semester: WS 2015/16, SS 2016

In this seminar students will get introduced to the topic of social robotics by reflecting upon this technological development through various sociological key notions such as visions, action, relations, otherness, boundary and culture. In addition the historical context, empirical cases and technical application will be discussed to support good understanding of the assumptions and attitudes found in social robotics. Overall, this seminar aims to equip students with the necessary skills to critically identify, analyze and discuss the many questions that social robotics raises for individuals and society.

Guest Lectures

I give guest lectures across Europe and in interdisciplinary programs on topics of trust, explainability, ethics, AI, and human-robot interaction. For example, I contributed to the ENFIELD MOOC series within the European ENFIELD project where I was covering the social and moral dimensions of user trust in AI. I have also taught theory-driven approaches to trust at TU Wien and RWTH Aachen University, as well as lectures on Explainable AI and ethics at the University of Salzburg. My guest lectures at Aalborg University and the University of Vienna integrated humanities perspectives, methodological rigor, and reflections on automation and AI. Across these experiences, my focus has been on connecting AI development with broader societal and ethical questions and encouraging students to see trust and trustworthiness aspects as central to AI system use and design.
